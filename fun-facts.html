Did You Know?

  • The sun is a giant fusion reactor!
  • Solar panels on the International Space Station power astronauts.
  • One solar farm can power thousands of homes.
  • Ancient Tech: Solar power isn't new! In the 7th century B.C., people used magnifying glasses to concentrate sunlight to make fire. Later, the Romans designed "sunrooms" in their bathhouses to trap solar heat (the first passive solar design)
  • Speedy Delivery: The energy from the sun that hits your solar panels left the sun about 8 minutes and 20 seconds ago. It traveled 93 million miles just to power your toaster!
  • Cloudy with a Chance of Power: Solar panels don't need direct blazing sunlight to work. They can still generate electricity on cloudy days and even in snowy weather (the snow reflects light, sometimes making them more efficient if the panels themselves are clear).
